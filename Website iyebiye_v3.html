I am Iyebiye Olatokun, a Brand and Communications Strategist based in Ibadan, Nigeria, with 5+ years of experience partnering with entrepreneurs, founders, and executives worldwide.

Before a single word goes on a page, I spend hours in research. I study your industry, your competitors, the culture your audience lives in, and the gap only your brand can fill. My goal is never just clarity; it is resonance so deep that your audience starts to feel something like loyalty, and eventually, something like fandom.

I am a lifelong learner, and that is not a personality trait. It is my method. The brands I am most proud of are the ones I understood so well that the words felt inevitable. From brand positioning frameworks to website copy, video scripts, ebooks, and social strategy, I have done the full range, and I bring that same research-first discipline to all of it. The Project
BeyondPlay Kidztown and Sensory Cove is a fully inclusive indoor play centre in Barrie, Ontario, built on the belief that play should be fun, purposeful, and developmentally enriching for every child, including those with sensory needs and disabilities. The founder reached out in late 2024 to build the brand and communications strategy for a space that did not yet exist. One year later, it is a thriving, booked-out play centre. The brief covered brand strategy, visual identity direction, website copywriting, social media strategy, and ongoing social media management.
The Research
The project started with hours of conversation with the founder, pulling apart her why before anything else was touched. From there came competitor research, audience mapping across parent and caregiver communities in Barrie and surrounding towns, and deep study of the culture around inclusive and purposeful play. Only once that foundation was solid did the strategy take shape. A four-step process guided the work: Empathize and Discover, Conceptualize and Define, Design and Develop, then Deliver.
The Work
I developed the full brand strategy and positioning document, with purposeful play at its core and fun as the anchor. Tone of voice and messaging pillars were built to speak directly to parents of young children as the primary audience, with the wider caregiver community and early childhood educators as secondary. I wrote the full website copy translating the brand vision into a parent-focused narrative, then designed and managed a three-phase social media strategy covering pre-launch awareness, grand opening, and post-launch community building.

The Project
Orisun Microfinance Bank was founded in 2001 in Ago Iwoye, Ogun State, as a community-first bank serving market women, farmers, small business owners, and salary earners. Two decades on, the bank was ready to modernise, expand its reach across Nigeria, and attract a younger, tech-savvy audience, without losing the grassroots trust that had defined it from the start. I came on as Brand Copywriter alongside Creative Director Tai Shittu-Alamu and Brand Designer Jude Babayemi.
The Research
The discovery phase involved deep stakeholder conversations, competitor analysis across Nigerian microfinance and commercial banking, and audience mapping across both existing community customers and the newer, digitally-native Nigerians the bank wanted to reach. Understanding the tension between legacy and ambition was the core research challenge, and it shaped every word that followed.
The Work
I developed the complete brand identity copy: vision, mission, core values, brand story, positioning statement, messaging framework, and tone of voice guide. The writing had to balance two truths at once, deep community roots and modern technological ambition, while remaining accessible to a broad Nigerian audience across generations. The result was a cohesive brand identity ready to support Orisun's expansion to a national scale.
The Project
Farntech Ventures is a private holding company that backs technology-driven financial businesses reshaping how money is managed, moved, and multiplied. Its portfolio includes Tranzfar (cross-border payments), Hustle (merchant payment solutions), Freedom (AI-powered banking), Bankfar (enterprise FX), and Apex (digital assets). As the company grew from a fintech startup into a structured, multi-vertical investment group, it needed brand language to match. I joined as Brand Copywriter alongside Creative Director Tai Shittu-Alamu and Designer Jude Babayemi.
The Research
Discovery sessions, competitor audits across holding companies and investment groups, and tone analysis shaped the research phase. The key finding was a perception gap: the existing brand felt like a startup; the business had already become something far more structured. The work needed to close that gap and position Farntech as a credible, disciplined institutional player with a clear long-term vision.
The Work
I wrote the full Farntech brand guidelines copy, including tone of voice, brand personality, messaging principles, and communication guidelines for consistent use across all platforms and sub-brands. The language needed to project clarity, discipline, professionalism, and quiet confidence without losing the ambition and forward-thinking energy at the company's core. Every element was documented to support consistency as Farntech scales.
The Project
FRWL New York engaged me at a genuinely early stage of their brand building. This is the kind of work I find most interesting: the moment before a brand fully exists, when everything is still possible and the words you choose will shape what it becomes. Brand story work done at this stage has an outsized influence on every piece of communication that follows.
The Work
I worked on the brand story and narrative for FRWL New York, developing the foundational written framework for how the brand would present itself, what it stood for, and how it would speak. The client noted that I provided much more than promised, listened to exactly what was needed, and exceeded expectations with the finished product.
